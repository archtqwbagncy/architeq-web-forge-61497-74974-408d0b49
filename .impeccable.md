# Design Context

### Users
South African home-services and local trades operators — roofers, HVAC, electricians, plumbers, construction, landscaping. Often owner-operators or small crews. They're hiring Architeq to turn their website into a working lead-gen channel: more phone calls, more booked jobs. They evaluate the site on phone, often in a truck or between jobs. Trust signals (real reviews, real phone number, plain pricing) matter more than abstract polish.

### Brand Personality
Three concrete words: **architectural, grounded, opinionated**. Voice is direct — no fluff, no SaaS jargon, no "elevate your synergy". Speaks like a foreman who happens to design websites. Confidence comes from specificity (7-day delivery, R-priced packages, named clients) not adjectives.

### Aesthetic Direction
Drafting-studio editorial. Warm paper background (not white), warm ink foreground (not black), single saturated **construction-ember orange** accent used sparingly. Oversized drafting numerals, hairline rule lines, tick marks, plate numbers ("PLATE 01 / 05") as section markers. Type pairing: **Bricolage Grotesque** (display, variable) + **Familjen Grotesk** (body). Both intentionally chosen against the AI-slop reflex list (no Inter, DM Sans, Outfit, Plex, Space Grotesk).

Anti-references: generic SaaS purple gradients, glassmorphism, laptop-mockup heroes, identical icon-card grids, gradient text, cyan-on-dark "tech" palettes, centered everything.

### Design Principles
1. **Paper, not screen.** Treat the page like a printed drafting sheet — rules, plates, tick marks, generous margins, asymmetric grids.
2. **Numbers do the talking.** Oversized tabular numerals carry hierarchy. Pricing, delivery days, project counts read first.
3. **One accent, used like a highlighter.** Ember orange marks only what's actionable or critical. Never decorative.
4. **Trades-literate copy.** "Get more calls", "Booked solid", "Quote-ready" — not "transform your digital presence".
5. **Mobile = phone in a truck.** Tap targets generous, phone number always one tap away, no horizontal scroll, no hidden nav.

### Technical notes
- Tokens remain HSL (Tailwind v3 wrapper assumes `hsl(var(--x))`). Values are warm-tinted toward the ember hue per the "tint neutrals toward brand" rule.
- `.gradient-text` legacy class kept as a solid color fallback so inner pages don't break.
